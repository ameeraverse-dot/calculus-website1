Power Rule

For f(x) = xⁿ, the derivative is given by:

d/dx [ xⁿ ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹

Example 1:

ddx [ x² ]

Step 1: Identify the exponent: 2

Step 2: Bring down exponent as coefficient: 2

Step 3: Subtract 1 from exponent: 2 − 1 = 1

Final Answer: 2x

WHY: According to the Power Rule, the exponent becomes the coefficient, and the new exponent is one less than the original. So the derivative of x² is 2x.

Example 2:

ddx [ x⁵ ]

Step 1: Identify exponent: 5

Step 2: Coefficient = 5

Step 3: Subtract 1 from exponent: 5 − 1 = 4

Final Answer: 5x⁴

WHY: Using the Power Rule, the exponent becomes the coefficient, and the new exponent is one less. So the derivative of x⁵ is 5x⁴.
